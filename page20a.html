A general rule to working out how much thinning your lens will  require :

· 0.00 – 3.00: Standard thickness

· 3.00 – 6.00: 1.6 lens

· 6.00 – 8.00: 1.67 lens

· 8.00 – 10.00: 1.74 lens

· 10.00 and above – glass lenses

Other Factors:

· Size of Frame – The further your eye sits from the centre of the frame, the thicker the lens edge of the lens will be.

· A big frame will require a bigger lens blank – this makes the lens thicker.

 

· This process will be applied vertically if you go for a deep lens.

 

· Exposed lenses will look thicker than they are.
